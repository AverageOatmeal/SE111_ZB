REVIEW

Zachary Baughan April 22nd, 2025

Monty Python is a very talented comedy group known for thier hilarious skits and witty humor. Thier work on Monty Python and the Holy Grail is no exception. In truth, the movie is effectively a collection of Monty Python skits with a central plot to tie them all together. While this would be a bold direction for any other movie, it only makes sense for a group like Monty Python, and they use the format to it's fullest.

The movie may not be everyone's cup of tea - if you prefer movies with deep lore to research and a dramatic story you'll be thinking about months later, Monty Python and the Holy Grail will likely fall short. On the other hand, if you're willing to embrace it's nonsensical and unorthadox nature, then perhaps you'll find yourself a new favorite movie in this masterpiece.

Trivia

  • Monty Python started performing back in 1969.
  • Most of the movie's extras were made up of tourists visiting the set and acting students from a nearby university.
  • A running gag throughout the movie is the follwing question "What is the air velocity of an unladen swallow?" The question goes unanswered throughout the movie. The correct answer, which is never stated, is about 20.1 miles per hour.
